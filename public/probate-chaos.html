Locked doors, overdue utility bills, property insurance lapses, and uncooperative heirs all add stress to your already full desk.

I CALM THE ROOM BEFORE IT GETS LOUD

Probate often starts quietly, but emotions can build fast. Family members disagree about price. Heirs question why things are taking so long. Executors feel overwhelmed and start calling their attorney for answers that are outside of your legal scope. Before long, all the frustration lands on your shoulders.

I have seen this happen countless times. That is why my first priority is to calm the room before it even gets loud. From the beginning, I explain what is happening, what the next steps are, and what each person’s role will be in plain and simple language.

Instead of leaving heirs confused, I show them a clear timeline of how the property will be handled. Instead of letting small disagreements turn into shouting matches, I clarify the process so everyone feels included and respected. By taking time to explain and set expectations, I remove the drama before it ever reaches you.

I TAKE THE PROPERTY OFF THE ATTORNEY’S DESK

Here is the truth: you should not be dealing with property tasks at all. Yet in probate, they almost always end up on your plate.

Who will meet the cleanout crew? Who will handle showings? Who is dealing with neighbors or a buyer’s questions about the property? If no one else steps up, the attorney gets dragged into it. That wastes your time and puts you in the middle of logistics that are not part of your role.

When I am on the case, all of that comes to me. If it involves a lock, a key, a showing, a yard, or a vendor, I handle it. You should never be copied on emails about insurance binders or junk removal bids. I make sure your client never feels abandoned, but you never get pulled into tasks that are not legal work.

This frees you to focus only on what you do best: protecting the estate legally and keeping the process compliant.

I GIVE THE EXECUTOR A CLEAR GAME PLAN

Executors are often new to the process. They may have never handled a probate case before, and they do not know where the attorney’s job ends and where someone else’s begins. So they call you for everything.

How do we change utilities?

How do we insure a vacant house?

When should we get photos?

Who do we hire for a cleanout?

These are not legal questions, but without guidance, the executor sends them to you anyway. That is where I step in.

From the very beginning, I walk the executor through the full real estate process. I explain each step, what needs to be done, and how it fits into the timeline. Instead of reacting to every question, I provide a structure they can follow. I do not just answer one-off calls, I create clarity.

Executors gain confidence. Families feel supported. And you stop getting interrupted with tasks that were never yours in the first place.

I DEFUSE PROBLEMS BEFORE THEY ESCALATE

Probate can feel like a minefield. Sometimes an heir is difficult, sometimes a buyer is shaky, sometimes a timeline is in danger of slipping. If no one is watching closely, these small issues can become big problems that explode in your lap.

I make sure that never happens.

When I see red flags, I raise them early. If an heir is pushing for unrealistic pricing, I prepare data to show real market conditions. If a buyer seems unstable, I check financing strength before we waste time. If a timeline looks like it might stall, I address it before deadlines are missed.
