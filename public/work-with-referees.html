I WORK WITH REFEREES SO YOU DON’T HAVE TO: HERE’S HOW THAT HELPS

One of the most overlooked delays in probate and trust real estate is the referee valuation. On the surface, it seems straightforward. A probate referee is appointed, they provide a valuation, and the case moves forward. But anyone who has worked on these cases knows it rarely plays out that simply.

Probate referees do not go inside the property. They complete drive-by valuations based on exterior views, public records, and whatever supporting data they are given. That means the accuracy of their report depends heavily on the quality of information provided to them.

If that information is incomplete, delayed, or inaccurate, the report can come back with problems that slow the case, frustrate the family, and force attorneys to spend more time than they should on clarifying values.

This is where I step in. My role is to support the referee with accurate, timely, and organized data so their work is clean and the case stays on track. Here is how I handle it.

I PROVIDE A COURT-APPROPRIATE CMA BASED ON REAL COMPS

A simple printout of MLS results is not enough. I create a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) that mirrors what buyers and appraisers are actually looking at today.

I pull local, condition-matched comparables instead of relying only on square footage or zip code.

I factor in lot size, interior condition, upgrades, deferred maintenance, and unique property characteristics.

I include supporting photos and notes that help explain the story behind the numbers.

This CMA is packaged in a way the referee can use directly, saving them time and giving them clarity. The result is a more accurate valuation that reduces disputes and delays.

I DOCUMENT INTERIOR CONDITION THE REFEREE CANNOT SEE

Because referees do not go inside, they miss critical information. A home may look fine from the outside, but inside it could be cluttered, outdated, or in serious disrepair. On the other hand, it may have been freshly cleaned out or updated, which significantly improves value.

I bridge that gap by documenting interior condition thoroughly.

I take clear photos of each room.

I create notes on repairs, upgrades, or deferred maintenance.

I flag anything that might influence buyer perception, financing, or appraisals.

By packaging this for the referee, I ensure they have a complete picture before writing their report. That means fewer surprises and a valuation that aligns more closely with reality.

I BRING IN SENIOR MOVE MANAGERS WHEN NEEDED

In some cases, the interior condition is complicated because of personal contents, disputes among heirs, or overwhelmed executors. This is where Senior Move Managers can make a difference.

I do not bring them in automatically. Instead, I evaluate whether they are needed.

If the executor is capable of handling contents and there is no conflict, I do not recommend adding expense.

If the heirs are in disagreement, or the executor is overwhelmed, I connect the estate with trusted Senior Move Managers who prepare full inventories, item logs, and organized reports.

These detailed inventories not only support the family but also help referees understand what remains inside, preventing valuation errors based on assumptions.

I MONITOR THE TIMELINE AND KEEP EVERYONE UPDATED

Referee delays often happen because no one is tracking the process. Once a referee is assigned, it can feel like a “black box” until the report shows up.

I do not let that happen.

I track when the referee was appointed.

I confirm when the report should be expected.

I provide regular updates to the executor and attorney so no one is left guessing.

If something looks delayed, I follow up directly.

This way, the legal team is never blindsided, and the executor feels confident the process is moving.

I FLAG RED FLAGS BEFORE THEY CREATE BIGGER PROBLEMS

Even with preparation, valuations sometimes come back looking off. Maybe the number is too high, too low, or does not reflect the property’s actual condition.

Instead of letting that stall the case, I double-check the valuation against the data we submitted.

If something seems missing or inconsistent, I provide a respectful, organized summary of the facts.

I equip the attorney with the supporting documentation they need to request clarification if they choose.

I never challenge the referee directly or step outside my lane, but I ensure the real estate side of the file is complete and accurate from day one.

FAQS

WHAT DOES A PROBATE REFEREE DO?

A probate referee provides an official property valuation for probate cases. They usually perform drive-by inspections and rely on public data and comps.

WHY DO PROBATE REFEREE VALUATIONS GET DELAYED?

Delays happen when the referee lacks complete information. Missing interior details, poor comps, or unclear communication can stall the process.

CAN A PROBATE REFEREE ENTER THE PROPERTY?

No. Probate referees typically do not enter the property. They perform drive-by inspections and depend on data provided to them.
